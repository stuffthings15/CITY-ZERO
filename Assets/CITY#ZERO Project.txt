Project Title

CITY//ZERO

One-Line Pitch

An original, top-down open-world crime sandbox set in a dense retro-modern metropolis, combining arcade chaos, systemic faction warfare, reactive police escalation, vehicle-based traversal, emergent missions, and a fully simulated urban ecology.

Core Vision

Build a commercially viable, original game inspired by the top-down open-world crime-action genre, but not dependent on any copyrighted game content. It must feel immediately readable, kinetic, replayable, systemic, and simulation-rich.

The game should deliver:

instant pick-up-and-play controls
deep emergent systems
layered progression
destructible, reactive city spaces
meaningful faction dynamics
vehicle chaos
police heat escalation
mission variety
high replay value
moddable data-driven architecture
a complete asset pipeline with original art and sound
Non-Negotiable Design Pillars
Readable from above
Every gameplay-relevant object must be legible at a glance.
Systemic over scripted
Systems should interact to create chaos without needing constant hand-authored sequences.
Fast arcade feel, deep simulation underneath
Easy to control, hard to master, rich in consequences.
Original IP
No copied names, districts, characters, gangs, logos, map layouts, vehicle silhouettes, mission scripts, or UI from any existing title.
Dense city life
Traffic, pedestrians, crimes, weather, transit, emergency services, faction conflicts, commerce, and rumors should all exist as interlocking loops.
Content scalability
Missions, NPCs, vehicles, weapons, shops, districts, and world events must be data-driven for rapid expansion.

SYSTEMIC EMERGENT GAMEPLAY

The game must support unscripted interactions between:

police
factions
civilians
traffic
weather
hazards
player actions
mission states

Examples:

player steals a gang van during rain, crashes through market stalls, alerts civilians, triggers police response, flees into rival faction turf, causing a three-way battle
player sabotages power in a nightlife district, disabling cameras and traffic lights, creating easier thefts but more chaotic collisions
player frames one faction by using stolen vehicles and weapon signatures tied to another faction

The design should prioritize these layered outcomes.
